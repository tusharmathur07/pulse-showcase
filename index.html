What Pulse Is

Pulse is a cash flow operating system. It connects to the tools owners already use — bank, accounting, payroll. It rebuilds the 90-day forecast every night from live data. And every Tuesday and Thursday at 8am, it sends a plain-English message — by SMS or email — with exactly what matters and exactly what to do. No login. No dashboard. The intelligence finds you.

Built in Vancouver. Built for Canadian businesses. Built by a founder who spent six months sitting across from business owners asking them to describe their worst financial moments — and then designed a product that prevents every single one of them.

Section II

The problem, in full.

These are not abstract market problems. They are the specific, documented, daily experiences of the people Pulse is built for. Each one is a failure mode the existing market has not solved.

1
The cash gap arrives without warning

A restaurant owner checks her bank account on Friday and realises payroll runs in four days — and she does not have enough cash to cover it. She has been watching QuickBooks for months. QuickBooks showed her last month's numbers. It showed her nothing about next week. The gap between "what happened last month" and "what will happen in 14 days" is where businesses die. Not from lack of revenue. From lack of timing.

2
Profitable on paper, out of cash on Tuesday

A restaurant does $80,000 in sales in March. Expenses are $72,000. The accountant confirms $8,000 profit. But a large catering invoice has not been collected. Payroll is in three days. The business is profitable and about to miss payroll. This phenomenon kills the best-run small businesses in Canada every quarter. It is not a management failure. It is an information failure.

3
The GST/HST surprise

Unlike income tax, GST/HST is collected continuously on every sale but must be remitted quarterly in a lump sum. Tens of thousands of Canadian businesses receive the remittance notice and discover they owe money they have not set aside. There is no financial tool at the SMB level in Canada that proactively tracks accumulated GST/HST obligations. Not one competitor.

4
Late payments getting worse

Xero's February 2025 data showed Canadian businesses waiting 7.6+ additional days beyond terms. CFIB reports cite late payments as the #1 cash flow stressor for Canadian SMBs. The agency owner with $87,000 in outstanding invoices who does not know which clients are slow — this is the documented daily reality for hundreds of thousands of Canadian businesses.

5
No early warning, no time to act

Float's 2025 Canada report found collateral requirements surged 43% year-over-year. Access-to-capital concerns hit 29% of small businesses. As credit gets harder, managing existing cash perfectly matters more. A business that knows about a cash gap 16 days in advance can act. A business that discovers it on payroll day cannot borrow their way out in 24 hours at reasonable rates.

6
Dashboards require effort owners don't have

Dashboards exist — Float, Dryrun, others. But they require the owner to remember to open them, navigate to the right screen, and interpret what they see. A restaurant owner working 70 hours a week does not check financial dashboards. The product has to come to the owner. Not the other way around. Fractional CFOs cost $2,000–$5,000/month — out of reach for the 33M businesses that need them most.

The six-step engagement model

From bank connection to your phone — what Pulse actually does, end to end.

01
Data ingestion
Pulse pulls fresh data nightly from your bank (Plaid), accounting (QB/Xero), payroll (ADP/QB Payroll/Gusto), and any POS or payment connections. Everything is normalised into a common schema. Errors, retries, and token refreshes are handled in the background.
02
Categorisation
Every transaction is tagged: payroll, rent, food cost, vendor, revenue, tax, software, equipment, owner draw. A three-layer system handles it — known merchants (40%, deterministic), Plaid ML (30%), and Claude fallback (30%) for ambiguous merchants.
03
Forecast construction
The core of the product. Pure Python — no AI. Deterministic arithmetic builds a 90-day projection: starting balance + every expected inflow (invoices, payouts, recurring revenue) − every expected outflow (bills, payroll, fixed costs). Each day has a confidence interval.
04
Detection
The forecast is scanned for the 15 metrics. Survival metrics check for hard breaches. Health metrics run z-score anomaly detection against the business's own 12-week history. Every triggered signal is scored for urgency.
05
Translation & validation
Top 2 signals are converted into structured JSON objects with all numbers pre-computed. Claude rewrites them into plain English. Then output validation runs: every dollar amount in Claude's response must match a number from the input JSON. If not, retry. No hallucinated number ever reaches you.
06
Delivery
Alerts arrive on your phone at 8am Tuesday and Thursday via SMS or email. Urgent signals (payroll risk within 7 days, cash gap within 7 days) fire immediately. Maximum two alerts per week — owner who receives seven ignores all of them.

Hire Affordability

Technical Specification

How Pulse actually works — under the hood.

A complete six-phase nightly pipeline, 14 PostgreSQL tables, 10 external integrations, and one architectural rule that protects every owner who uses the product.

Deterministic Python math handles all financial calculations. The LLM (Claude) handles only natural language. These two responsibilities never overlap. A number that appears in any alert was computed by the Python model before the LLM ever saw it. The LLM receives a structured JSON object. It writes sentences from those numbers. It cannot invent numbers it was not given. This is the core safety guarantee of the entire product.

The nightly pipeline — six phases, every business, 2–4am

The pipeline runs every night between 2am and 4am Vancouver time for every active business. Six sequential phases. Each reads from the database and writes back. If any phase fails for a specific business, that business is marked failed and engineering is paged. Other businesses continue unaffected.

P1
Data ingestion
Python + AWS Lambda, scheduled 2:00 AM. Fires API calls to Plaid, QB/Xero, ADP/Gusto/QB Payroll, Square/Toast, Stripe/Shopify. Normalises responses into common schema.
Input
Raw API responses from external services
Output
Clean records in PostgreSQL: transactions, invoices, bills, payroll_runs, daily_balances
P2
Categorisation
Three-layer classification: (1) Rules engine for known merchants — 40%, zero latency. (2) Plaid ML category mapping — 30%. (3) Claude fallback for ambiguous — 30%, ~85% first-pass accuracy. User corrections feed rules engine.
Input
Raw transactions table
Output
Each transaction tagged with category, confidence, is_fixed_cost, is_recurring
P3
Financial model — 90-day forecast
The core of the product. Pure Python + NumPy. No AI. Deterministic. Validated against 6 months historical data before production. Builds: projected_balance[day] = current_balance + Σ(inflows[1..day]) − Σ(outflows[1..day])
Input
Categorised transactions, open invoices, outstanding bills, payroll schedule, bank balance
Output
daily_forecast[1..90]: projected balance + confidence interval per day
P4
Detection
Scans the 90-day forecast for all 15 metrics. Survival: hard threshold breach. Health: z-score against business's own 12-week history. Each triggered signal becomes a structured object with severity, urgency, evidence, and resolution options.
Input
90-day forecast + categorised transaction history
Output
Ranked signal objects, top 2 selected for delivery
P5
Translation & delivery
Constructs JSON alert objects with all numbers pre-computed. Claude rewrites as plain English. Output validation: every dollar amount in Claude's response must match an input number (±$50 tolerance). If validation fails, retry. Twilio or SendGrid sends.
Input
Top 2 scored signal objects
Output
Delivered alerts on owner's phone by 8am Tuesday and Thursday
P6
On-demand Q&A
Triggered by an owner asking a question in the app. Sub-5-second response. Three-step: Claude #1 parses intent → Python scenario engine re-runs Phase 3 with modifications → Claude #2 writes the answer from pre-computed numbers. Same output validation as P5.
Input
Owner's natural language question
Output
Plain-English answer with specific dates and amounts

What you can hold us to. The principles we built the product on.

Every architectural decision in Pulse flows from a small set of commitments to the people who use it. These are not marketing language. They are constraints the product is built around.

1

2
We will never store your banking credentials. Plaid handles your bank connection. We store only a secure access token. You can revoke our access at any time in three seconds.

A note on accuracy

Pulse's forecasts are as accurate as the data that feeds them. The two most common sources of inaccuracy are: accounting data that is not up to date (invoices entered late, transactions miscategorised) and bank connections that have gone stale.

We actively monitor both. If your QuickBooks data is more than 48 hours stale, you receive a notification. If your Plaid connection breaks, we send a re-authentication request before your data goes stale — not after. Every forecast is labeled with its data freshness so you always know what you're looking at.

Frequently asked questions

Is my data safe?

Yes. Plaid handles bank connections — we never see your banking credentials. All data is stored in AWS Montreal. Everything is encrypted at rest and in transit. SOC 2 Type II compliant with annual penetration testing. You can revoke Pulse's access at any time.

What if Pulse sends a wrong alert?

Every alert has a "Was this accurate?" feedback button. Wrong alerts are reviewed within 24 hours. The output validation layer prevents hallucinated numbers from reaching you. Accuracy is tracked publicly and improves every week.

Do I need to change my accounting?

No. Pulse works with your existing QuickBooks or Xero setup. We read your data. We never write to it or change it. The only thing that improves accuracy is keeping your books reasonably current — which is good practice regardless.

Can my accountant use Pulse too?

Yes. The accountant portal lets your accountant view your Pulse data — the same forecasts and alerts you see — so they can use it in advisory conversations. Particularly useful if you have a fractional CFO or tech-forward accounting firm.
